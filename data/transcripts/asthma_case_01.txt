Doctor: Do you have a history of asthma?

Patient: Yes, I was diagnosed about six years ago.

Doctor: Have you had any recent exposure to dust, smoke, pollen, or anything that usually triggers your asthma?

Patient: Yesterday I was cleaning my house, and there was a lot of dust.

Doctor: Have you used your rescue inhaler?

Patient: Yes, I used it twice, but it only helped a little.

Doctor: Have you had fever or chest pain?

Patient: No, just wheezing and difficulty breathing.

Doctor: Are you taking any regular asthma medication?

Patient: Yes, I use a steroid inhaler every day.

Doctor: Let me check your vital signs.

Doctor: Your temperature is normal. Your oxygen saturation is 95%, pulse is 102 beats per minute, and I can hear wheezing in both lungs during examination.

Doctor: This appears to be an asthma exacerbation, likely triggered by dust exposure. I'll prescribe a nebulizer treatment and advise you to continue your inhalers as directed. Patient: Thank you, doctor. I'll follow the treatment plan.
