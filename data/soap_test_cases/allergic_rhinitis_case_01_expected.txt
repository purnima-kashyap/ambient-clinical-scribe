# SOAP Note

## Subjective

* Chief Complaint: Sneezing and runny nose for 5 days.
* History of Present Illness:

  * Frequent sneezing.
  * Clear nasal discharge.
  * Itchy, watery eyes.
  * Nasal congestion.
  * Symptoms worsen outdoors and during the morning.
  * Previous similar seasonal episodes.
  * Partial relief with an over-the-counter antihistamine.
* Past Medical History: No significant medical history.
* Allergies: No known drug allergies.

## Objective

* Temperature: Normal
* Blood Pressure: 116/74 mmHg
* Pulse: 80 bpm
* Oxygen Saturation: 99%
* Physical Examination:

  * Swollen nasal mucosa.
  * Clear nasal discharge.
  * Throat normal.
  * Chest clear on auscultation.

## Assessment

Allergic rhinitis, likely triggered by environmental allergens (dust/pollen).

## Plan

* Start oral antihistamine.
* Prescribe intranasal corticosteroid spray.
* Avoid dust and pollen exposure.
* Keep windows closed during high pollen periods.
* Follow up if symptoms persist or worsen.
